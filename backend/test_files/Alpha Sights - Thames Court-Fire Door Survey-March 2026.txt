Executive summary & scope  
Article 17 of the Fire Safety Order makes it a legal requirement to ensure that fire resisting doors and escape doors are correctly installed and adequately maintained in order for them to be fit for 
purpose. The authorities have the power to enforce the Fire Safety Order and do prosecute or even close buildings down where breaches are discovered. Building owners need ‘competent persons’ 
as referenced in the Fire Safety Order to help them comply with fire door regulations.  
EAS Interiors were contracted to carry out a detailed fire door survey at AlphaSights Thames court – Levels 2 & 3 Tennant doors. This inspection was carried out on all fire doors to determine the 
effectiveness of the doors in their current condition. Each door was inspected for overall condition, fitting, fire and smoke seal condition, door furniture condition and the frame condition with seals. 
This report details the outcome of the survey for each door and any recommendations to ensure action is taken to resolve any issues that may breach the safe working of the door as a fire/smoke 
barrier or escape route.  
Door sets inspected on all floors 
39 door sets in total. 
 
6 compliant door sets. 
 
31 non-compliant door sets – Remediation needed to bring these to compliance.  
2 Door set require replacement  
No O&Ms for the door sets were provided for this survey. 
The fire strategy drawings were not provided by the client. 
This survey was non-intrusive, and the architraves were not removed due to destructive nature of works required. Therefore, the fire stopping between the frame and wall was not inspected.

EAS Interiors have conducted the following Fire Door Maintenance survey under the BM TRADA 3rd party certification scheme. Scope of the 
BM TRADA Q-MARK fire door scheme:  
The purpose of the BM TRADA Q-Mark fire door maintenance scheme is to provide independent 3rd party certification for the activity of taking a fire-resistant door 
set that needs adjustment and/or has minor to moderate damage, and to repair or maintain it in such a way to restore the fire resistance performance to what it was 
before the damage occurred or the door set became non-compliant.  
The scheme does not provide certification for either the upgrade or door sets to improve their fire resistance, or the survey of door sets to determine their period of 
fire resistance. If the period of resistance needs to be determined, this should be undertaken by an appropriate expert prior to any maintenance work being carried 
out by the individual certified under the scheme.  
Prior to any maintenance activity being undertaken, the responsible person must identify which door sets are required to be fire resistant and what the period of fire 
resistance is for each door set. This will normally be established through a fire risk assessment, with the fire-resistant properties of the door sets being confirmed 
through a review of fire test evidence to BS 476 Pt 22 or BS EN 1634. Where it is available, documentation confirming the period of fire resistance of each door set is 
to be proved by the client. Where such documentation is not available, any inspection or maintenance activities carried out will only be against the schemes general 
rule of thumb, and the level of confidence in such an inspection will be considerably lower.  
All fire-resistant door sets which will require maintenance must be confirmed by the client in writing in advance, indicating the declared fire resistance for each door 
set, with the location of each door set being clearly identified on floor plans.  
The maintainer does not take any responsibility for confirming whether the risk assessment is acceptable, for determining the period of fire resistance that is 
required for any door sets or determining the period of fire resistance that any door set is likely to achieve.

Fire Door Certification  
Fire doors are specially engineered and designed to contain fire for a period and work as part of a layered fire protection system within a building. Fire doors require test evidence 
or an assessment to prove they will meet their intended fire rating, to stop the spread of fire and smoke.  
When fire doors are supported by assessment/test sheets and certification such as this, it is possible to carry out suitable remedial actions to restore a door set to a manufacturer 
specified specification, providing a heightened confidence of the fire doors performance.  
Provided that the maintainer carries out replacement of hardware and the adjustment of door sets in accordance with assessment sheets, ensuring that the door meets the 
manufacturers assessed specification, the door will perform as required.  
In the absence of these labels or plugs to indicate a fire door’s intended fire resistance, it is difficult for a surveyor or maintainer to determine whether it meets the required 
fire resistance for the wall in which it is situated. Typical assumptions can be made under the Q-Mark scheme to rule out a fire door from achieving a given fire resistance 
but cannot be used to determine the actual fire resistance of the door set.  
In these circumstances, the responsible person will be required to take responsibility for the intended fire resistance of the door set requiring remediation.

Fire Door Signs Regulations.  
It is the responsible person onsite responsibility to ensure all Fire Doors must have “Fire Door Keep Shut” signs on both sides of the door. If a set of double doors, 
both doors should have signs on both sides. Some fire doors will have automatic door closers fitted (connected to the fire alarm system) so the signs will say 
“Automatic Fire Door Keep Clear” or similar. Door sets without closers need to be locked and can have a single “Fire Door Keep Locked” sign on the outside of 
the door.

Action Level & Timeframe Summary 
Level 
Action Priority 
N/A 
Not applicable or compliant. 
Low 
Low level risk in a non-critical location. Low level non-compliance with remediation(s) or replacement(s) recommended within 12 months. 
Moderate 
In a location deemed moderate risk usually within or linked to an evacuation route. Moderate non-compliance with remediation(s) or replacement(s) recommended within 9-12 months. 
High 
In a location deemed as high risk usually in a principal position. High level non-compliance with remediation(s) or replacement(s) recommended within 3-6 months. 
Critical 
In a location deemed as critical and in a principal position. Critical level of non-compliance with remediation(s) or replacements(s) recommended within 3 months. 
Replace 
Unable to remediate to make compliant and needs replacement.

3 
No documentary evidence other than manufacturer identifier marking or labelling showing fire rating and certificating body.
